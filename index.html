Overview

HouseMate is a mobile app based on the the dynamics of housesharing. Designed to delegate household tasks, organise payment of bills and streamline communication between housemates, with the goal of simplifying these processes and reducing interpersonal conflict experienced within share houses.

Research Ideation Wireframes Design Testing Prototype

Research

Our research consisted of a combination of methods, including face to face interviews and a smaller sample sized version of the questions, sent out in an online survey, we focused on the following objectives.

  • To learn how housemates delegate household chores between one another
  • To understand how housemates organise the splitting and payment of bills
  • To find out how housemates keep updated with all social events and urgent household issues.

    • Over the course of the two days we conducted six 1 on 1 interviews with users who are currently living in share-house accommodation or have done in the past 12 months. A smaller sample of questions was sent via a facebook survey to participants who met the criteria, these two methods allowed us to collect both quantitative and qualitative data to identify the common pain points/themes that users have brought up and try to find possible solutions.

    Cleaning Schedule

    The responses were positive for having a cleaning roster to assist in delagating household tasks between house mates

    Responsibility

    Results pointed towards the majority of people attend to household duties at least once a week.

    House finances

    We were suprised to see the majority have their bills included in the rent, opposed to splitting them evenly.

    During our user interviews and online survey, we discovered that people living in share houses like to live in a clean house but don't like to take initiative and the responsibility of delegating tasks. There was a negative perception of the role "House Mum", refering that "telling people what to do, can lead to creating resentment and tension within the house". This was interesting, as most people had mentioned that the most frustating and annoying part of house sharing is when house mate's don't help with cleaning up or a messy individuals. This was a large part of repsondenants negative house sharing experiences, as users did not want to bring it up out of fear it would create conflict amongst housemates.

    Ideation

    With the research wrapped up, the team got together to anaylyze and start to deconstruct the data collected. The affinity diagram allowed us to notice patterns and themes amongst the user insights. Using this method was vital in helping us discover the users needs and pain points, whilst offering possible solution options to think about.

    The image is a sample size of the full affinity diagram to display the topics created from our user insights. The topics are based on the 5 recurring themes we discovered, which are important to our users' experience in relation to share housing. The 5 factor are:

    - Cost: Share housing is attractive as it means expensives are shared so it is a great way to save money

    - Responsibility: Taking initiave and being on top of household tasks

    - Cleaning: How often do you clean and how do you feel about a cleaning roster?

    - Interpesonal: Communication between housemates, how do they keep up to date with house hold matters?

    - Community: the overall enviroment and dynamic between housemates. Is it friendly or is there tension around?

    After synthesizing our data, we discovered people felt that living in a clean house and sharing the responsibilities of household tasks, created a more harmonious community environment, which was the goal for people who live in a share house. With this goal in mind we were able to define the problem we were designing a solution for:HouseMate is designed to create a harmonious community environment within a share-house. By simplifying delegation of tasks to avoid interpersonal conflict amongst housemates, allowing our users to co-exist in share-house living and create better relationships amongst all housemates.

    The Problem

    We believe equal distribution of household responsibilities and creating accountability for housemates within share houses will achieve a harmonious community environment for all.

    The Solution

    HouseMate is designed to create a harmonious community environment within a share-house. By simplifying delegation of tasks to avoid interpersonal conflict amongst housemates, allowing our users to co-exist in share-house living and create better relationships amongst all housemates.

    Feature Prioritisation

    We decided to focus our efforts on solving user problems such as cleaning rosters, dividing expenses among housemates, and having a space where all housemates could communicate and update with special events and live in a harmonious environment.

    Story Board

    The goal was becoming clear, we had to design a solution to allow users to create a community environment within the share house and simultaneously make sure all household responsibilities were evenly shared and completed.

    User Flow

    Wireframes

    Onboarding

    Early drafts of the onboarding screens included the option to log in or sign up on 3 of the 4 coaching screens. to give the user the quick option to opt out of a coaching screen and create an account. Users can also personalise their house details during the onboarding.

    Home Screen

    A key feature idea was to be able to have a bulletin board style homepage that alerted users of upcoming household matters. Users can log on and interact with the app daily, by being able to see if there's any tasks that require to complete or messages from housemates.

    Key Features

    The real inspiration behind the app and the feature we believe will add the most value to our users experience. The principles applied to the design of this page was to make sure it was clean and easy to read, whilst displaying all the necessary information for the user.

    Testing

    With a mid-fidelity prototype built, it was time to have the design tested to learn how users interact with HouseMate and gain some valuable feedback on the features that have been implemented.

    Home Page

    Users felt like it was a duplicate of information on the feature pages. By simplifying the notice board to small tiles with quick views allowed the user to see all necessary information on the home page and could navigate to the page if they wanted more details.

    The Home Icon used for the settings/profile edit wasn’t clear to users. Changing the Home Icon to the setting icon allows users to clearly identify how to navigate to the main home screen and not the house profile.

    Onboarding

    The log in options in the coaching screens were confusing to users. We decided to remove the log in options from the home screen and have a main log/sign up page at the end of the coaching screens

    Create the option to name the house during the onboarding process. This was a valuable piece of feedback which was a high priority during the iteration process because we understood it gave the users a more personalised experience and the naming of the house could be a fun shared activity.

    Final Prototype

    Conclusion

    Overall though the team is very happy with the result of the project and feel we have designed an app that can add real value to its users lives. Currently with share housing being popular we believe there is a large market to capitalize on and hopefully it will allow us to assist our users in achieving their end goal of creating a harmonious community environment within their share house.
